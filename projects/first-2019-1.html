FIRST 2019 (FTC)

This was the first robot I built for the FTC (FIRST Tech Challenge) robotics competition. The challenge that year was called "Rover Ruckus," and it involved retrieving objects from the ground, which could be balls or cubes, and placing them inside a spacecraft located in the center of the field at a height of at least twice the robot's original height.


This robot didn't have many advanced features as it was my first experience with many of the components, but within its limited capabilities, it had the following features:

  • Mecanum wheels for omnidirectional movement.
  • Gear reduction arm for manual control.
  • Intake made with rubber bands for adaptability to different game elements.
  • Second motor for hanging the robot and avoid straining the thin gears of the arm.

This robot advanced to the national elimination rounds for a design award and made it to the semifinals.
