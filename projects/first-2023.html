FIRST 2023

This robot will never be built as I am no longer part of any FRC team, but I wanted to continue honing my 3D modeling and rendering skills by creating this concept of a competitive robot capable of reaching the finals of a world championship. The design style is similar to what I have used in previous years, but it incorporates higher quality engineering and simulations to ensure its proper functionality.


The robot is designed to be built by a team with medium-high manufacturing capabilities and strong knowledge in electronics and programming.


Below are the features that this robot would have if it were to be built:

  • Low center of gravity.
  • Retractable intake using small pneumatic pistons.
  • Flexible indexer to work with both cones and cubes.
  • 100% aluminum arm.
  • Arm lock with a 20 amp motor.
  • Horizontal arm extension with gravity lock using a 10 amp limited current on a motor.
  • 180-degree rotating claw to orient cones.
  • Linear-motion claw with pistons.
  • Differential drive chassis to reduce costs.
  • Determination of the position of the game element within the robot through analysis of current graphs in the motors.
  • Absolute encoders for the position of the arm and orientation of the claw.
  • Magnetic limits for the linear elevator.
  • Mechanical limits and integrated encoder on the elevator to determine whether the claw should pick up a cone or a cube.
  • Encoders on the chassis for position estimation.

The design can be found on my GrabCAD.
