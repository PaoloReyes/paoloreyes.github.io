E-Bike

For a period of my life, I dedicated myself to selling products online and making home deliveries. The challenge was that I had to make the deliveries on foot as I didn't have a car. Later, I decided to switch to making deliveries by bicycle. However, I found it tiring, so I decided to convert my bicycle into an electric one using motors I had at home and the control system from the competition robots I participated in. The process was challenging due to limited tools and budget, but somehow, I managed to get it up and running.


The electric bicycle had the following components:

  • Three brushless NEO motors.
  • Three SPARK MAX motor controllers to control the speed by PWM pulses.
  • An arduino UNO as the brain of the bike.
  • A potentiometer connected to a brake lever that served as an accelerator.

The bicycle worked flawlessly and served me well for indefinite travel for nearly 3 months, until I had to return the components to the robotics team since they were not mine.
